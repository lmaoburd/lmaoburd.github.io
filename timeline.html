Timeline

Birth

April 5, 1955

Borned in the town of Kiyosu, Aichi Prefecture, Japan

Early Inspirations

1955 - 1975

Some of Toriyama earliest inspiration include an animated film One Hundred and One Dalmatians(1961)

and a Japanese manga series by Osama Tezuka, Astro Boy

Early Debut

1977 - 1978

Participated twice in the Monthly Young Jump Awrad sponsered by Shueisha with Awawa World in 1977 and Mysterious Rain Jack in 1978 but did not win.

A few months after Torishima's phone call, Toriyama made his first published manga, Wonder Island. Published in Weekly Shōnen Jump

Dr. Slump

1980 - 1984

achieved his first great success, Dr. Slump. He first gained fame from this manga, which was published in Weekly Shōnen Jump from January 1980 to August 1984.

Marriage

May 2, 1982

married Yoshimi Katō on May 2, 1982. She is a former manga artist from Nagoya under the pen name "Nachi Mikami" and occasionally helped Toriyama and his assistant on Dr. Slump when they were short on time.

They had two children: a son named Sasuke born on March 23, 1987, and a daughter named Kikka born in October 1990.

Dragon Balls & International Success

1984 - 1995

Dragon Ball was one of the linchpins for what is accepted as the "Golden Age of Jump". The success from Dragon Ball encouraged Akira Toriyama to continue working on the series from 1984 to 1995. During that 11-year period, he made 519 chapters collected into 42 tankōbon volumes. Each volume has an average of 200 pages, so the entire Dragon Ball storyline extends to almost 9,000 pages.

Other success

1985 - 2008

co-authored Hetappi Manga Kenkyūjo, a manga in which he explains how to create a manga.

Akira Toriyama's clean line and design sense led to jobs designing characters for the phenomenally popular Dragon Quest series

he created the puppet characters of a short film titled Apple Pop that was shown in April in Hirake Ponkikki

produced his first animated film, Kosuke & Rikimaru - The Dragon of Konpei Island

he started a new manga series titled Cashman - Saving Soldier which was published in V-Jump. A sequel to Cashman - Saving Soldier

Other Works

2008 - 2024

he received a special award at the 40th Angoulême International Comics Festival in France. As part of the 45th anniversary celebration of Jump, Toriyama made a manga series titled Jaco the Galactic Patrolman, which debuted in the July 13, 2013 issue of Weekly Shōnen Jump

Death

March 1, 2024

Toriyama died on March 1, 2024 at the age of 68 from acute subdural hematoma
